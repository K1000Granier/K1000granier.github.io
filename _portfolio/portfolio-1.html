--- title: "Hybrid-Vlasov 3D3V Turbulence" excerpt: "3D3V hybrid-Vlasov simulations of magnetosheath-like turbulence, electron-only reconnection, and ion-electron heating.
" collection: portfolio --- In this work, we performed 3D3V hybrid-Vlasov simulations, with kinetic ions and fluid electrons, of plasma turbulence driven near ion scales under magnetosheath-like conditions. The objective is to understand how sub-ion-scale energy injection controls ion and electron heating, and how reconnection emerges in this regime.

The simulations show a β-dependent partition of dissipated energy between ions and electrons, in agreement with MMS measurements. At small scales, the cascade transitions toward electron-dominated dynamics, consistent with a kinetic Alfvén to whistler regime.

Within this turbulent environment, thin electron diffusion regions, fast electron jets, and weak ion response arise self-consistently, producing electron-only reconnection events similar to those observed in the magnetosheath.

This work provides a direct link between in-situ observations, reduced plasma descriptions, and kinetic simulations, and clarifies how sub-ion-scale turbulence controls both heating and reconnection in space plasmas.
